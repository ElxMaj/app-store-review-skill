# App Store Rejection Recovery: Nimbus Weather

## Problem Description

Petra Kovač, an independent iOS developer, received a Guideline 4.3 rejection from Apple for her weather app, Nimbus Weather. The app took approximately 6 weeks to build and was rejected less than two hours after submission. Petra is unsure what her next step should be and needs a well-structured response to send to Apple's Resolution Center.

The rejection notice and full app details are in `inputs/rejection-details.md`. Read that file carefully — it contains Apple's verbatim rejection message, the app's version and build information, and a description of the app's features.

Petra needs your help to understand what type of rejection she received, what her options are, and what to actually say to Apple. She does not want to make things worse by saying the wrong thing.

## Output Specification

Produce two files in the working directory:

1. **`analysis.md`** — A structured analysis of the rejection that includes:
   - What type of 4.3 rejection this is and what it means for the recovery path
   - A classification of the recommended response type
   - An assessment of what evidence Petra can legitimately rely on
   - Any risks or things Petra should avoid doing or saying

2. **`resolution-center-reply.md`** — A ready-to-send draft of the message Petra should submit in the App Store Resolution Center. The draft should be appropriately concise, professional, and actionable. It should include placeholders for any attachments or credentials Petra would need to supply, and it should include the exact navigation path or reference information Apple will need to locate the build.
